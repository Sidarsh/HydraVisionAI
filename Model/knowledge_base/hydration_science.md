# Hydration Science

Skin hydration refers to the water content of the epidermis and the stratum corneum.
Proper hydration ensures the skin remains supple, elastic, and intact as a barrier.
Transepidermal Water Loss (TEWL) is the rate at which water evaporates from the skin. High TEWL indicates a compromised skin barrier, leading to dehydration.
Natural Moisturizing Factors (NMFs) such as amino acids, urea, and lactic acid naturally bind water in the skin.
