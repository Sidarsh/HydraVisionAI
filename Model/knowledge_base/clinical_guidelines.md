# Clinical Guidelines

Dermatological guidelines for assessing skin hydration status:
- A tight, drawn feeling is the most common subjective symptom of dehydration.
- Scaling, roughness, and fine lines (often called "dehydration lines") are clinical signs.
- Treatment focuses on barrier repair using a 3:1:1 ratio of ceramides, cholesterol, and free fatty acids.
- Avoid hot water showers as they strip the skin of its natural lipids.
- Humidifiers are recommended in dry or air-conditioned environments to prevent environmental water loss.
